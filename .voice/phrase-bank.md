## Usage rules

- **One phrase per page**, ideally. Two if the page is long enough that they don't compete.
- Display statements live in `--text-display-md` or `--text-display-lg`.
- Always uppercase. Always with periods. The periods are part of the brutalism.
- Never quote them in body copy — they live as standalone visual moments only.
- Don't translate. They work in English globally. On the ES site they sit untranslated as visual statements (the surrounding copy is Spanish). The mix is the point.

## Adding new phrases

A new statement earns inclusion only if it:

1. Stands alone without context
2. Works in `--text-display-md` or larger
3. Has under 5 words
4. Uses the imperative or declarative — no questions, no qualifiers
5. Survives being read aloud without sounding like a deck slide

If it fails any of these, it's a body copy line, not a phrase bank entry.

## Anti-pattern check

These would NOT make it in the phrase bank (examples of what NOT to add):

- ❌ `LET'S BUILD SOMETHING AMAZING.` — generic, "amazing" is empty
- ❌ `THE FUTURE IS NOW.` — meaningless, slogan-y
- ❌ `INNOVATING TOMORROW, TODAY.` — banned word + nothing said
- ❌ `THINK DIFFERENT.` — already taken, also not yours
- ❌ `WHERE DESIGN MEETS CODE.` — "where X meets Y" is a tired construction

When in doubt, leave it out. The bank stays sharp by staying small.
