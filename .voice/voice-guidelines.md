# Voice & Tone Guidelines

> Read before writing any copy. Applies to ES and EN equally.

## Who's writing

Juan Cruz Luna. Argentine product designer & builder. Buenos Aires. Reads as a person, not a brand. Knows tech, grew up in design, has opinions, doesn't pretend.

## Voice attributes

**Conversational with absurdist humor.**
Like talking to a designer friend who happens to be honest about the work and a little weird. Non-sequiturs are welcome. Self-aware, never self-important.

**Confident without bragging.**
State the result, skip the adjectives. "Led design at LDP" beats "passionately leading transformative design experiences at LDP".

**Honest about doubt.**
"Built with doubt" is in the phrase bank for a reason. Showing you don't know everything = senior signal. Reflection sections in cases earn more interviews than confident outcomes.

**Anti-corporate.**
This is a personal site, not a deck for board approval. Speak like a person who has read books and shipped things.

## Language strategy

### Spanish (default)
Rioplatense. "Vos" not "tú". Tech terms in English when natural — *"hice un audit del DS"*, *"armé un onboarding flow"*, *"shippeamos en 3 sprints"*. Never translate "design system" to "sistema de diseño" — sounds forced.

### English
Not a neutral translation. Same humor, same attitude, in English. Sometimes the joke lands differently — rewrite, don't translate. If a Spanish phrase only works in Spanish, leave it untranslated and italicize.

### Toggle behavior
The `/en/*` version is its own draft, not a clone. Both versions audited separately for voice. The English site is not "the international version" — it's the same person writing in another language.

## Banned words and phrases

Never use, in any language. If you find yourself reaching for one, the underlying idea is wrong, not the word.

- `apasionado` / `passionate`
- `soluciones` / `solutions` (as a generic word; OK if it's literally the solution to a specific problem)
- `user-centric` (as an empty adjective)
- `sinergia` / `synergy`
- `innovador` / `innovative`
- `disruptivo` / `disruptive`
- `journey` (in "user journey" specifically — too overused; OK for actual travel)
- `mindful` (when meaningless)
- `crafted with love`
- `soy un eterno curioso` / `I'm a lifelong learner`
- `holistic`
- `delightful` (when meaningless — fine if a specific delight is being described)

**Tag of warning:** if your sentence still works after removing the word, the word was decoration. Cut it.

## Phrases that signal the voice

Pulled from the actual phrase bank — use as tonal reference, never as filler:

- *"Diseño productos digitales. Y a veces los rompo a propósito."*
- *"El formulario de contacto es un email. Anti-fricción máxima."*
- *"Built with doubt."*
- *"Designers who build build better."*
- *"Pixels are cheap. Decisions aren't."*

## Editorial rules

1. **Open with the verb, not the qualifier.** *"Diseñé un onboarding"* beats *"Tuve la oportunidad de diseñar un onboarding"*.
2. **Numbers over adjectives.** *"40% menos pasos"* beats *"much faster"*. Use real numbers when you have them. Don't invent them when you don't.
3. **Names over roles.** *"Trabajé con Mati en backend"* beats *"Collaborated with the engineering team"*. Names humanize, roles abstract.
4. **One idea per sentence.** Brutalist editorial demands rhythm.
5. **Periods are free. Use them.**
6. **Avoid "we" inflation.** Don't claim team work as solo work. Don't claim solo work as team work.
7. **Edit out 30% of the first draft.** It's always too long.
8. **Read it out loud.** If it sounds like an "About" page on a corporate site, rewrite.

## Length guidance

- **Hero subhead:** 1 sentence, max 14 words.
- **Case Hero outcome:** 1 sentence, max 18 words.
- **Case Context:** 2-3 paragraphs, max 90 words each.
- **Case Problem:** 1 paragraph, max 80 words.
- **Case Approach:** 1-2 short paragraphs.
- **Case Key Decision:** 80-150 words per decision.
- **Case Reflection:** 1 paragraph, max 120 words. Be honest, not exhaustive.
- **About bio:** 3 paragraphs max.

If you're going long, you're padding.

## When in doubt

Read the line out loud. If it sounds like Juan talking to a friend in a café, ship it. If it sounds like a LinkedIn post, rewrite.
